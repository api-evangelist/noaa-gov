Sea turtles breathe air like all reptiles and have streamlined bodies with large flippers, making them well-adapted to ocean life in tropical and subtropical waters worldwide. Of the seven sea turtle species globally, six inhabit U.S. waters: green, hawksbill, Kemp's ridley, leatherback, loggerhead, and olive ridley turtles.
